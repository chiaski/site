Chia


as a whole, kind of

Chia (b. 2000, tondo) is an internet artist in radical pursuit of the web, words, & worlding by the systems that shape them. Based in New Haven and studying CS & Art at Yale, by way of Manila. Previously, they've coded & designed at Spotify, Kumu, Rappler, for NDMOs, literary publications, and many other startups & communities. Incoming Product Designer at Figma.

Now focusing on making, music, and community building. Nurturing Filipino technologists at Developh, leading Design at Yale & Friends of Figma at Yale, crafting an artsy browser-based game maker, and launching an experimental press/label/whatever out of my studio apartment. Also writing a book (mostly a compilation with some new pieces).

Now

In New Haven, Connecticut.

Seeking work in music (labels, distribution, publications, tooling, community) before I start my full-time job in August 2022.

Working on my thesis: an artsy gamemaker inspired by maps, retro tech, 'walking simulators', and Dwarf Fortress.

Launching Bad Internet, a music blog. The usual criticism is nice, but I'm moved by the challenge of musical ekphrasis (especially by people you care about) and emotion spurred by music. I'm excited about leading an interview series highlighting visual + live art/designers that work closely with musicians.

Working on maybe, an experimental press + label to publish & create things for myself and my friends. Writing and designing a collection of essays. Producing artists' books and zines. (You can pick some of these up here!)

Collecting vinyl & cassettes, making tape loops, re-learning piano, playing with synth-y ambient sounds and the worst noise music you've heard. I want to soundtrack my own indie games & net art + apply this later to software (e.g. Warioware).

I want to collaborate and create! If you would like to make something together, reach out to me.

I would like to spend my days figuring out how we can enable others to create. Until the act of creation is truly ubiquitous, software will not serve us and art will not speak to us. In the long-term, my biggest dream is evolving Developh into a nonprofit physical community space, school, and press in Manila exploring the intersections of tech, design, and the arts.
